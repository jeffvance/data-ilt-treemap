    Revised Abstract

    The technology landscape related to "data" and "data technologies" has dramatically changed over the last 10 years. Today the "buzz" around "Big Data" is stronger than ever.

    In this session, we will talk about the "taxonomy" we are using internally within Red Hat so that we can continue to refine our strategy. As we continue to refine our strategy with respect to "big data" at Red Hat, we found that we needed some kind of common "framework" to help us make decsions (prioritize potential product or partner strategies, etc). Our hypothesis is that many people attending Red Hat Summit will be partially familiar with the extent to which the technolgoy and vendor landscape has been changing, and suspect that there maybe some broader interest and applicability for the the framwork we are using. We hope this will help you understand the complex web of technologies that make up the big data ecosystem, or at least provide you with a way to think about how these technolgies may impact you and your company. In todays talk we will mostly focus on publicly available data from a third party that provides some insight into the relative "popularity" of various "database technolgoies". The overall technolgoy landscape for data is much broader than just "databases", but we will focus mostly on databases because there are an astonishing number of new technolgies just within "databases" and because there is useful, public data available.

    NOTE

    This is "work in progress" at Red Hat, so this talk is in some sense preliminary. We will talk about some potential next steps for Red Hat in this area, and we are interested in feedback from that can potentially help use shape our next steps (perhaps to help address the specfic questions that we cannot answer today with the data and research available).

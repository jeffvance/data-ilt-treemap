Overall "Data" Taxonomy

Ideal would be...

Ideally, there would be a single, standard, "taxonomy" that neatly describes the entire "Data" landscape. If such a "taxonomy" existed we would have a standard set of "categories", with straight forward, definitions for each category. It would be easy for customers or developers to figure out which technologies solve which problems, and which vendors provide which technologies. So assuming customers and developers understand the problems they have, life would be good.

Reality Check...

The status quo doesn't resembled the above ideal state. There is not single "taxonomy" for data. New technolgoies and new vendors pop up (literally) daily. The rate of change is not constant or uniform - some areas are more mature and stable - but there is a general lack of clean/standard categories even for the older more mature parts of the data landscape.

Industry analysts both help solve this problem and contribute to it. Individual industry analyts to a great job of coming up with frameworks to help their clients understand what's going on - and they sell this insight. That's the good news. The bad news is that they rarely agree on the specific categories, etc.

Vendors also contribute to the "problem". Vendors inherrently strive to position their products in a way that that sets them apart from their competition. They often position their products as being "comprehensive" addressing customer problems that transcend multiple categories. Vendors (generally) continuously innovate - thus constantly adding features and functions that potentially blur the lines with respect to which category or categories their product(s) fit in.

So we don't have one taxonomy for "Data" we have many. All of them (probalby) have merit, some are (undoubtedly) better than others.
